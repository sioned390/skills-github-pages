Penmon Point

Scenic Stroll to Penmon Point

Perfect for you and your pup and a 5-star review from us! If you're looking for a dog-friendly walk with breathtaking coastal views, a touch of history, and a cozy café at the end, then Penmon Point is an absolute must. Goonie, and I recently took this walk, and it was a wonderful experience for both of us.

The Walk from Penmon Priory:

We started our walk from Penmon Priory, following the path towards Penmon Point. It’s about 1.7 miles (2.8 km), mostly easy-going, with a mix of paved paths and natural terrain. There were a few uneven sections, but nothing too challenging—Goonie managed just fine, taking in all the new scents along the way! The views get better with every step, with glimpses of Puffin Island and the distant Great Orme as you get closer to the coast.

Toll Fee for Car Access:

Since Goonie is getting on in years, we opted to drive up to the car park at Penmon Point instead of walking both ways. There’s a small £3.50 toll fee to access the area by car, but it’s worth it for the convenience and the stunning scenery awaiting you at the end.

Dog-Friendly Café & Treats!

After our walk, we popped into the Pilot House Café, a welcoming little spot right by the shoreline. It’s dog-friendly, which is always a bonus! Goonie was in for a treat—dog-friendly ice cream (which he thoroughly enjoyed), while I had a warm cup of tea and a relaxed game of Monopoly. Sitting outside with the fresh sea breeze and views of Trwyn Du Lighthouse was the perfect way to unwind.

Overall Verdict:

Penmon Point is a fantastic spot for a leisurely dog walk, whether you choose to walk up from the Priory or drive and enjoy a shorter stroll. The mix of history, coastal views, and a dog-friendly café makes it an ideal outing for both humans and their four-legged companions. If your pup loves sniffing out new adventures, I’d highly recommend it!

Difficulty

Easy: We usually take the path from Penmon Priory to the lighthouse and find this path the best for Goonie.

Distance

The walk from Penmon Priory to Penmon Point is approximately 1.7 miles (2.8 km) and is considered easy, typically taking around an hour to complete. However, there are many different paths that lead to Penmon Point which are more difficult in terrain and distance.

Terrain

The route offers a mix of paved sections near the priory and more natural terrain as you approach the coast. While generally accessible, some areas may have uneven surfaces, so it's best to wear comfortable footwear.

Location

For directions, enter Beaumaris LL58 8RP or Penmon Point into Google Maps or any navigation software. Or head back to the map to view the address pin.
