Aberlleiniog Castle

Nestled amid the enchanting landscapes of the Isle of Anglesey, Castell Aberlleiniog stands as a testament to medieval fortifications, an impressive motte and bailey fortress crafted by the hands of Hugh d'Avranches, 1st Earl of Chester, between 1080 and 1099. Our recent visit, accompanied by our ever-enthusiastic canine companion Goonie, was an adventure through time and terrain. The walk to this historic site was delightfully manageable, although the muddy paths added a touch of rustic charm to our journey. However, our anticipation was met with a slight disappointmentâ€”the castle itself was closed to visitors, a factor that nudged our rating down a star. Despite this, the experience was steeped in the rich history and natural beauty of the area, making it a worthwhile escapade for history buffs and dog-walkers alike. As for things to do nearby, Penmon Point, another of our favorites, is a 10-minute car ride away. As for food and drink, a local Morrisons is a stone's throw away from the car park, and Beaumaris is a 10-minute drive for a variety of restaurants and cafes.

The castle is known for its impressive stone walls and towers, which have been well-preserved over the centuries. Visitors can explore the ruins and learn about the history of the castle and its role in Welsh history.

Difficulty

Easy: This walk is perfect for a leisurely stroll, although do expect mud if it's been raining. The walk also has parts of boardwalk and very few hills. The stairs, however, are steep, but railings are provided all the way to the top.

Distance

This one's up to you. From the car park to the castle, the walk is 0.06 miles. However, you may continue through the woodland for a circular walk and come out at the top of Llangoed. This walk is around 1.91 miles.

Terrain

The terrain mostly consists of woodland, boardwalk, stairs (to the castle), and mud!

Location

For directions, enter Llangoed, Beaumaris LL58 8RN or Aberlleiniog Castle into Google Maps or any navigation software. Or head back to the map to view the address pin.
